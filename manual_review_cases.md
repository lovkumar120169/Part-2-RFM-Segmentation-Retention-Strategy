
# Manual Customer Review Cases Report

## Objective

This document highlights customers where automated segmentation alone is insufficient for making retention decisions. These customers display conflicting behaviors such as high revenue with poor experience, strong engagement with low purchases, or frequent purchases with declining activity.

These cases require manual CRM review before launching retention campaigns.

---

# Case 1: Customer ID - CUST00045

## Customer Profile

- Segment: High Value but Unhappy
- Monetary Value: Very High
- Purchase Frequency: High
- Support Tickets: Multiple complaints
- Return Rate: Elevated

## Why the Decision Is Difficult

The customer contributes significant revenue but has experienced repeated service issues. Providing discounts alone may not solve the underlying dissatisfaction.

## Recommended Action

Assign a dedicated customer support specialist to resolve all unresolved issues. Offer a personalized apology, replacement assistance, and premium support experience.

# Overall Manual Review Recommendation

Automated segmentation provides efficient customer prioritization, but complex cases require human judgment. CRM teams should focus on customers with conflicting signals where revenue potential and dissatisfaction appear simultaneously.

Priority should be given to high-value customers with negative experience indicators because recovering these relationships delivers the greatest financial impact.
