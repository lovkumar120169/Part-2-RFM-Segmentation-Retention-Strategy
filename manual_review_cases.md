# Manual Customer Review Cases Report

## Objective

This document identifies customers for whom automated segmentation alone is not sufficient to make effective retention decisions. These customers exhibit mixed behavioral patterns, such as strong revenue contribution combined with poor customer experiences, high engagement with limited purchasing activity, or frequent purchases followed by declining interaction.

Such cases should undergo manual CRM evaluation before any retention initiatives are executed.

---

## Case 1: Customer ID - CUST00045

### Customer Profile

**Segment:** High Value but Unhappy
**Monetary Value:** Very High
**Purchase Frequency:** High
**Support Tickets:** Multiple complaints
**Return Rate:** Elevated

### Why the Decision Is Difficult

This customer generates substantial revenue but has encountered recurring service-related issues. Simply offering discounts may fail to address the root causes of dissatisfaction.

### Recommended Action

Assign a dedicated support representative to address all outstanding concerns. Provide a personalized apology, replacement assistance where appropriate, and an enhanced customer support experience.

# Overall Manual Review Recommendation

Automated segmentation is highly effective for prioritizing customers, but complex customer scenarios still require human evaluation. CRM teams should focus on customers exhibiting conflicting signals, particularly where strong revenue potential exists alongside indicators of dissatisfaction.

Highest priority should be assigned to high-value customers showing negative experience signals, as successfully retaining these relationships can generate the greatest business and financial impact.
