
# Customer Retention Strategy Report

## Project Overview

This report explains the customer segmentation strategy developed using RFM analysis combined with additional behavioral signals including support complaints, return behavior, website engagement, and discount dependency.

The objective is to allocate limited retention resources efficiently by targeting customers with the highest potential business impact.

---

# Segment 1: Champions

## Customer Behavior

Champions represent the most valuable customers in the business. They purchase frequently, spend significant amounts, and have very recent transaction activity.

They generally demonstrate:
- Low recency values indicating recent purchases.
- High purchase frequency.
- High total monetary contribution.
- Very low return rates.
- Minimal customer support complaints.

## Recommended Retention Actions

Retention efforts should focus on maintaining loyalty instead of providing unnecessary discounts.

Recommended campaigns:
- Early access to new product launches.
- VIP membership benefits.
- Personalized product recommendations.
- Exclusive reward programs.
- Priority customer support services.

## Expected Business Value

Protecting Champions maintains a significant percentage of company revenue because these customers contribute disproportionately high lifetime value.

---

# Segment 2: Loyal Customers

## Customer Behavior

Loyal Customers purchase consistently and have strong engagement with the brand but have lower value compared to Champions.

They generally demonstrate:
- Good purchase frequency.
- Healthy spending behavior.
- Recent purchase activity.
- Limited customer complaints.

## Recommended Retention Actions

The business should encourage these customers to increase purchase frequency and become Champions.

Recommended campaigns:
- Product bundle recommendations.
- Loyalty point multipliers.
- Cross-selling campaigns.
- Personalized category suggestions.
- Limited-time loyalty rewards.

## Expected Business Value

Increasing the value of Loyal Customers provides sustainable revenue growth with lower marketing acquisition costs.

---

# Segment 3: High Value but Unhappy Customers

## Customer Behavior

These customers generate high revenue but display negative experience signals such as high support tickets or excessive product returns.

They generally demonstrate:
- Strong historical spending.
- High complaint frequency.
- Higher return behavior.
- Increased risk of switching to competitors.

## Recommended Retention Actions

This segment requires immediate human intervention because losing these customers creates significant revenue loss.

Recommended campaigns:
- Personal customer support calls.
- Dedicated complaint resolution.
- Refund or replacement prioritization.
- Service recovery rewards.
- Personalized satisfaction surveys.

## Expected Business Value

Successful recovery preserves high-value customers and improves brand reputation through better customer experiences.

---

# Segment 4: Discount Sensitive Customers

## Customer Behavior

These customers purchase regularly but depend heavily on promotional discounts before making transactions.

They generally demonstrate:
- Moderate purchase frequency.
- Strong response to promotions.
- Higher average discount utilization.
- Lower willingness to purchase at full price.

## Recommended Retention Actions

The business should reduce dependency on aggressive discounting while maintaining customer engagement.

Recommended campaigns:
- Personalized coupons.
- Bundle pricing strategies.
- Reward points instead of direct discounts.
- Category-specific promotional offers.
- Subscription-based savings plans.

## Expected Business Value

Managing this segment improves profit margins while preventing unnecessary revenue loss through excessive discounts.

---

# Segment 5: Dormant and At Risk Customers

## Customer Behavior

These customers have very old purchase histories, low engagement, or limited historical spending.

They generally demonstrate:
- High recency values.
- Low transaction frequency.
- Low monetary contribution.
- Weak website engagement.

## Recommended Retention Actions

The objective is to reactivate valuable dormant customers while controlling marketing expenditure.

Recommended campaigns:
- Win-back email campaigns.
- Time-limited promotional offers.
- New product announcements.
- Customer feedback surveys.
- Personalized re-engagement messages.

## Expected Business Value

Reactivating existing customers is often less expensive than acquiring entirely new customers.

---

# Campaign Budget Prioritization Strategy

Because the company has a limited retention budget, campaigns should be prioritized based on expected revenue impact and recovery probability.

## Priority 1: High Value but Unhappy Customers

Reason:
- They already generate significant revenue.
- They display clear dissatisfaction signals.
- Immediate intervention can prevent major revenue loss.

Recommended Budget Allocation: 40%

---

## Priority 2: Loyal Customers

Reason:
- They have high potential to become Champions.
- Upselling opportunities provide long-term revenue growth.

Recommended Budget Allocation: 25%

---

## Priority 3: Champions

Reason:
- Retaining top customers protects existing revenue streams.
- Low-cost loyalty programs are usually effective.

Recommended Budget Allocation: 15%

---

## Priority 4: Dormant and At Risk Customers

Reason:
- Some customers can be recovered through targeted campaigns.
- Broad spending should be avoided due to uncertain returns.

Recommended Budget Allocation: 15%

---

## Priority 5: Discount Sensitive Customers

Reason:
- Excessive discount spending can reduce profitability.
- Retention should focus on value-based incentives.

Recommended Budget Allocation: 5%

---

# Final Business Recommendation

The company should avoid applying identical retention campaigns to all customers. Data-driven segmentation enables personalized actions, improves customer satisfaction, protects high-value relationships, and maximizes return on retention investment.
